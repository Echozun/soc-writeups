# Humans as Attack Vectors

**Source:** TryHackMe — SOC Level 1 path
**Date:** 2026-06-12

## Key Concepts

**The human element.** It's generally far easier to exploit a person than to find and exploit a vulnerability in a system. Attackers know this, which is why social engineering is so heavily used.

**Attacks on humans.** These attacks work by manipulating the victim into helping the attacker — designed to look trustworthy and to play on emotion. Social engineering is the most common form. Deepfakes (AI-generated audio or video built to impersonate someone the victim trusts) are a newer technique that's increasingly being used.

**Defending humans.** A SOC analyst's role here is detection and investigation. Defense breaks into two tasks: mitigation and detection. For the human element specifically, security awareness training is the most effective tool available.

## What was actually new

Nothing about the core concepts was new — phishing and social-engineering attacks almost always target emotion, and that's consistent with what I've seen investigating these alerts in practice: it's rarely the technical sophistication that gives them away, it's the language. The one thing worth flagging is the increasing use of deepfakes as an attack vector — a newer wrinkle on the same underlying tactic.
