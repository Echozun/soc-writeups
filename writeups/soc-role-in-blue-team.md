# SOC Role in Blue Team

**Source:** TryHackMe — SOC Level 1 path
**Date:** 2026-06-11

## Key Concepts

**Security hierarchy.** Priorities and structure vary by company, but the general chain runs executives (CEO) → security leadership (CISO) → security managers (SOC Manager) → technical roles (analysts, engineers). Knowing where a given decision or escalation actually needs to land in that chain matters as much as making the right call in the first place.

**The Blue Team.** Defensive security — monitoring and responding to alerts — isn't one role, it's a set of roles and sub-departments working together. An L1 analyst is one piece of a larger structure, not the whole defense.

**Advancing a SOC career.** The room covers entry paths into a SOC (internal team vs. MSSP) and what typically comes next from there.

## Situation and Decision

The room's exercise: act as CISO for a fabricated company and assign each stage of an attack chain to the role responsible for handling it. This one was a straightforward role-mapping exercise rather than an investigation — match each attack-chain stage to the team that owns it. Nothing about the assignments themselves required real deliberation; the value was in the hierarchy concepts above, which is the part that actually maps to escalating things to the correct teams in a real SOC.
